# Caption Examples — Annotated

Three real WAT.ai captions across different post categories, with explanations of the voice patterns at work in each. Use these as the calibration reference when generating new captions.

---

## Example 1 — Specific role recruitment

**Category:** Application opening for a specific leadership role (Technical Project Manager).
**Energy:** Medium. **Emoji density:** None.

### Prompt that would generate this
> Announce that Technical Project Manager applications are open for Summer 2026. Apply by March 29th. Rolling review. TPMs lead 8-month projects with teams of 4–8.

### Caption (verbatim)
> We're excited to launch our Technical Project Manager Applications for new Summer 2026 Projects!
>
> Apply by March 29th at https://forms.gle/qJ82fWBgwN47vZbV6 (link in bio). We review candidates on a rolling basis.
>
> Technical Project Managers lead independent project teams. Each project runs for 8 months and is composed of 4-8 members.
>
> Apply if you have a cool project idea and want funding for an MVP while building leadership and technical skills!

### Annotations
- **Opener uses the canonical `We're excited to launch our…` pattern.** This is one of the recurring openers — reuse it for similar role-recruitment posts.
- **No emoji at all.** This is normal for recruitment of a specific leadership role — keeps it slightly more serious than the broad-recruitment voice. Emoji density is calibrated to audience: a TPM applicant pool is smaller and more targeted, so the post doesn't need to shout.
- **Inline URL + `(link in bio)` redundancy.** Caption posts on Instagram strip clickable links from the body, but the URL is included anyway as a copy-and-paste fallback. The `(link in bio)` parenthetical handles the standard Instagram pattern.
- **`We review candidates on a rolling basis` is a value signal**, not just info — tells applicants they don't need to wait or rush, which improves application quality.
- **Concrete program details** (8 months, 4–8 members) appear in their own paragraph. Always include duration + team size for any project/program post.
- **Closer is benefit-focused, not aspirational.** "Funding for an MVP while building leadership and technical skills" tells the applicant what they'll *get*, not what we *value*. Specific > inspirational.
- **Two exclamation points total** — one at the opener, one at the closer. Bookends.

### Generalizes to
Any specific-role recruitment: project lead, executive, design lead, research lead. Default to no emoji and the `We're excited to launch our…` opener.

## Example 2 — Technical event / industry collab

**Category:** Speaker event with an external partner.
**Energy:** Medium. **Emoji density:** None.

### Prompt that would generate this
> Announce a collab with Unsloth AI. Daniel Han, cofounder of Unsloth, is hosting a session on Tue Nov 25th at 6 PM online. Topic: how RL finetuning works and whether it scales to AGI.

### Caption (verbatim)
> WAT.ai is excited to announce a collab with Unsloth AI! Unsloth AI is the most popular package for RL finetuning opensource LLMs. Next Tuesday Nov 25th at 6 PM, we'll host Daniel Han to explain the basics of how this works and the limits of scaling this approach to let LLMs improve towards AGI. If you're interested in attending, signup here: https://tally.so/r/kddROM

### Annotations
- **Opener uses the third-person variant `WAT.ai is excited to announce…`** instead of `We're excited to…`. Use the third-person variant when introducing a collaboration or partnership — it positions WAT.ai as a named entity meeting another named entity.
- **Single paragraph, no breaks.** Technical event posts often run as one continuous block. Don't force paragraph breaks for short captions.
- **Partner is contextualized in one sentence:** "the most popular package for RL finetuning opensource LLMs." Always explain who/what the partner is in plain language — don't assume the reader knows.
- **Speaker name + topic stated together** ("we'll host Daniel Han to explain…"). Standard pattern for any speaker event.
- **No emoji.** Technical content in front of a partner brand → restrained voice. The collab itself is the energy; emoji would dilute it.
- **CTA pattern: `If you're interested in attending, signup here: [url]`.** Reuse this exact phrasing for any event signup.
- **No `(link in bio)` redundancy here** — possibly because this was an online-only event and the inline URL is sufficient. For in-person events, add `(link in bio)`.
- **Topic is described with a hook**, not just a title. "How this works and the limits of scaling this approach to let LLMs improve towards AGI" frames the talk as a question, not a lecture description. Good model to follow.

### Generalizes to
Any speaker event, panel, workshop with an external speaker, or industry collab. Default to no emoji and the third-person `WAT.ai is excited to announce…` opener when there's a partner brand involved.

## Example 3 — Broad student recruitment

**Category:** Open call for general membership across multiple project teams.
**Energy:** High. **Emoji density:** Heavy.

### Prompt that would generate this
> Announce that Core Member applications are open for Fall 25. 7 practical projects available. Apply by September 20th. All experience levels welcome.

### Caption (verbatim)
> 📢 CALLING ALL UW STUDENTS INTERESTED IN AI! 🤖✨
>
> WAT.ai is excited to announce that Core Member applications are now open! 🚀 We have 7 practical projects for you to gain AI experience. For more details, apply here 👉 https://forms.gle/g3YmHuYj3df8Fig3A by September 20th. Link in bio as well!
>
> We can't wait to see your skills in action, and we encourage you to apply to any project — no matter your experience level. 💡🙌
>
> Good luck, and we look forward to welcoming you to the team! 🔥

### Annotations
- **ALL-CAPS hook line with megaphone emoji** opens the post. This pattern is reserved for *broad-audience* posts where the goal is reach across the whole student body — not for targeted role recruitment.
- **Emoji are clustered at natural line breaks**, not sprinkled inside sentences. `🤖✨` ends the hook, `💡🙌` ends the inclusive line, `🔥` ends the closer. This is the WAT.ai pattern — emoji as paragraph terminators.
- **The pointer emoji `👉` precedes the URL.** Standard CTA pattern: `apply here 👉 [url]`. Reuse this verbatim for high-energy CTAs.
- **Inline URL + `Link in bio as well!`** — same redundancy pattern as Example 1, but more enthusiastic phrasing.
- **`no matter your experience level`** is the recurring inclusive language. This phrase (or a close variant) appears across any post that wants to lower the application bar. Reuse it in broad-recruitment posts.
- **Closer pattern is two parts:** good-luck wish + welcome statement. `Good luck, and we look forward to welcoming you to the team!` Use this exact pattern for general application posts.
- **Concrete number** ("7 practical projects") appears even in the high-energy version. Even when emoji-heavy, the voice always grounds itself in specifics.
- **Em-dash appears once** (`apply to any project — no matter your experience level`). Em-dashes are fine but not signature here; commas and exclamations carry most of the rhythm.

### Generalizes to
Any broad-audience, lower-the-bar recruitment: term kickoff posts, "applications open" for anyone-can-apply roles, general callouts. Default to high emoji density and the ALL-CAPS hook line.

## Quick reference — voice across the three examples

| Trait                    | Ex 1 (TPM)       | Ex 2 (Unsloth)        | Ex 3 (Core Member)        |
| ------------------------ | ---------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Opener pattern           | `We're excited…` | `WAT.ai is excited…`  | ALL-CAPS hook + `WAT.ai…` |
| Emoji count              | 0                | 0                     | 8                         |
| Exclamation count        | 2                | 1                     | 5                         |
| Paragraph count          | 4                | 1                     | 4                         |
| `(link in bio)` used     | yes              | no                    | yes (`Link in bio as well!`) |
| Closer style             | benefit-focused  | none (CTA is closer)  | good luck + welcome       |
| Concrete details present | yes (8 mo, 4–8)  | yes (date, time, who) | yes (7 projects, deadline)|

**Pattern:** the three captions share structure (opener → body → CTA) and recurring phrasing, but flex emoji density and exclamation count by audience breadth and post energy. When generating, decide audience breadth first → that determines emoji density → then fill in the standard structure.
