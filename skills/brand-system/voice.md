# WAT.ai Voice — Captions & Microcopy

This file documents the voice WAT.ai actually uses in Instagram captions today. Generated captions should match this voice.

> Note: this file describes existing patterns. It is not a prescriptive "better" voice — it's a snapshot of how WAT.ai posts read on Instagram across categories. If the voice changes in the future, update this file to match.

---

## Voice in one paragraph

WAT.ai's caption voice is **enthusiastic, accessible, and student-friendly**. It assumes the reader is a UW student who might be technical or might not be, and it works to lower the bar to participation. It uses exclamation points freely, emoji for warmth and emphasis, and a recurring set of opener phrases ("We're excited to announce…", "WAT.ai is excited to…"). Captions explain *what something is* in plain language and end on an encouraging note. They are not compressed or em-dash-heavy — they read like a friendly group message, not a magazine pull-quote.

---

## Standard caption shape

Most captions follow this loose structure (not rigid — varies by post type):

1. **Opener** — announces what the post is about, often with `We're excited to…` / `WAT.ai is excited to…`. Optional emoji or ALL-CAPS hook line above for high-energy posts.
2. **Body** — 1–3 sentences explaining the program, event, or person. Concrete details: dates, durations, team sizes, names, what's covered.
3. **CTA** — link, deadline, or signup URL. Often inline + `(link in bio)` redundancy.
4. **Closer** — encouragement (`we can't wait`, `good luck`, `apply if you…`, `we look forward to…`). Optional.

Length: 50–120 words typical. Single paragraph or 2–4 short paragraphs.

No hashtag block at the end (none of the existing captions use hashtags).

---

## Punctuation & casing

- **Exclamation points** are normal punctuation, not reserved for excitement. 1–4 per caption is typical.
- **Emoji** are used at variable density depending on post energy:
  - Restrained announcements (collab, alumni, technical content): 0 emoji.
  - Standard announcements (applications, projects): 0–2 emoji.
  - High-energy callouts (broad recruitment, term kickoff): 4–8 emoji, often clustered at line breaks (`🚀✨`, `💡🙌`, `🔥`).
- **ALL-CAPS hook lines** are acceptable for high-energy posts (`📢 CALLING ALL UW STUDENTS INTERESTED IN AI! 🤖✨`). Use sparingly — at most once per caption, at the very top.
- **Sentence case** for body content. Title Case for program names (`Core Member Applications`, `Technical Project Manager`, `RL to AGI`).
- **Em-dash** is fine but not required. Most captions use commas and exclamations instead.

## Recurring phrases & openers

These show up repeatedly across captions and are part of the voice. Reuse them where they fit.

**Openers:**
- `We're excited to launch our…`
- `We're excited to announce…`
- `WAT.ai is excited to announce…`
- `Announcing a collab with…`
- `📢 CALLING ALL UW STUDENTS…` (high-energy variant)

**CTA phrases:**
- `Apply by [date] at [url]`
- `(link in bio)` / `Link in bio as well!`
- `If you're interested in attending, signup here: [url]`
- `For more details, apply here 👉 [url]`
- `We review candidates on a rolling basis.`

**Closers:**
- `Apply if you…` (followed by a benefit)
- `We can't wait to see your skills in action`
- `Good luck, and we look forward to welcoming you to the team!`
- `…no matter your experience level.`

**Inclusive language:**
- `no matter your experience level`
- `we encourage you to apply to any project`
- `whether you're [X] or [Y]`

## Tone by post category

Voice flexes by post type. Match the energy to the category:

| Category                           | Energy   | Emoji density | Example tone                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------- | -------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Broad student recruitment          | High     | Heavy (4–8)   | "📢 CALLING ALL UW STUDENTS! 🚀✨ We have 7 practical projects…"             |
| Specific role recruitment (TPM)    | Medium   | Light (0–2)   | "We're excited to launch our Technical Project Manager Applications!"        |
| Technical event / collab           | Medium   | Light (0–1)   | "WAT.ai is excited to announce a collab with Unsloth AI!"                    |
| Alumni / spotlight                 | Medium   | Light (0–2)   | "Meet [name], WAT.ai co-founder and now…"                                    |
| Workshop / educational content     | Medium   | Light (0–2)   | "Workshop 01 of Spring 26 — Zero to ML. Eight weeks, Tuesdays 6–8 PM…"       |
| Recap / wrap-up                    | Warm     | Medium (2–4)  | "What a term! 🙌 Thanks to everyone who came out to…"                        |

When in doubt, default to the **medium / light-emoji** voice — it covers most posts.

## Things this voice does *not* do

These are tics the existing captions avoid. Don't generate them:

- **Long hashtag blocks.** No `#ai #ml #datascience #python #tech #waterloo #engineering #stem`. Existing captions have zero hashtags.
- **Marketing-deck language.** No "leverage," "synergy," "cutting-edge," "next-generation," "unlock potential," "empower."
- **Vague hype without substance.** Every caption includes concrete details — dates, team sizes, what's covered, what's required. Don't write "Big things coming!" with no info.
- **Cold corporate tone.** The voice is warm and student-led. "We" not "the organization." Friendly closers, not just URLs.
- **Cross-promotion of unrelated brands or platforms.**

---

## Microcopy on graphics

On-graphic text follows the *graphic* style guide (`tokens.md`, `motifs.md`), not the caption voice. Graphics are tighter and more compressed:

- Graphic: `deadline: Sept 20th @ 11:59 pm`
- Caption: `Apply by September 20th — link in bio!`

Both are correct for their surface. Don't apply caption voice to graphic microcopy or vice versa.
