# Annotated Examples

Three reference posts with explanations of *why* the design choices work. The goal is not for new graphics to imitate these layouts, but to understand the principles so new layouts feel native.

---

## Example 1 — "Zero to ML" (Workshop poster)

**Category:** Event / learning content → **particle scatter** background.

**What's happening:**
- Top-left gold metadata: `• Spring 26 · Tuesdays 6-8 PM` — answers *when* immediately.
- Top-right muted system tag: `WORKSHOP · 01` / `SPRING_26` — places the post in a series without competing for attention.
- Hero: `Zero to ML` in outlined display serif, two lines, occupying the upper-middle 60% of the canvas. The outline treatment lets the particle-scatter background bleed *through* the letterforms, integrating type into atmosphere.
- Pill tag: `• E7/PSE 2409` — a single pinpoint detail, called out with the only gold-outlined shape in the layout.
- Bottom-left chevron list: topics covered, two columns, monospace, with `›` markers.
- Bottom-right: WAT.AI lockup.

**Why it works:**
- The outlined serif title is conceptually right — "Zero to ML" is a *concept*, not a person or an announcement. Outlined treatment matches abstract content.
- Negative space in the lower-right quadrant is deliberate — it's where the particle scatter is densest, and filling it with text would suffocate the atmosphere.
- The two-column chevron list is functional but doesn't compete with the title because monospace type at small size reads as system metadata, not as competing content.
- Gold appears in exactly three places: top-left dot, pill outline + dot, and the W-mark. Roughly 5% of visible color. Restraint.

**Anti-pattern this avoids:** filling the empty space with a generic illustration or stock graphic.

## Example 2 — "Core Member Applications Open"

**Category:** Application / deadline → **grid mesh** background.

**What's happening:**
- Watermark layer: `Core Member` faded at top, `Applications` faded at bottom — outlined serif at ~12% opacity. Creates depth and reinforces the message without adding information.
- Center top: WAT.AI lockup + `Spring 26` system tag, in monospace. Establishes context above the hero.
- Hero block, two parts:
  - `[ Core Member ]` — outlined serif wrapped in literal bracket characters. The brackets mark this as a *system entity* (a role, a slot to fill).
  - `Applications Open` — solid white heavy sans, in a black filled box behind it. This is the *announcement*, the active verb.
- Bottom: `deadline: May/June xth @ 11:59 pm` — gold monospace in lowercase. The casual lowercase + `xth` placeholder is the WAT.ai voice.

**Why it works:**
- Two heroes is normally a sin, but here they're functionally distinct: the outlined serif names *what* (Core Member), the solid sans says *what's happening* (Applications Open). The eye reads them as a single phrase, not two competing elements.
- The grid background signals "this is a process / form / structure," reinforcing the application context.
- Watermark repetition makes the canvas feel layered and intentional rather than empty.
- All metadata is gold monospace — total color discipline.

**Anti-pattern this avoids:** using a button or call-to-action graphic. The *typography* is the CTA.

## Example 3 — "Alumni Spotlight: Jarett Dewbury"

**Category:** People / achievement → **constellation graph** background.

**What's happening:**
- Top-left gold monospace: `ALUMNI SPOTLIGHT` with extra letter-spacing — the section header.
- Top-right: `WAT.AI · BIOMEDICAL ENGINEERING` in monospace, with a faded `01` in muted gold behind it (entry number in the series).
- Left edge: thin gold vertical accent bar — a marker rail, not decoration.
- Hero: `JARETT DEWBURY` in solid heavy sans, ALL CAPS, white. Two lines.
- Below hero: `→ MIT + ICLR` in gold monospace — the headline achievement.
- Below that: two-line monospace detail in muted white — the supporting bio.
- Bottom-left: WAT.AI lockup. Bottom-right: `WATAI.CA`.

**Why it works:**
- A name is a *who*, so it takes the solid heavy sans treatment, not outlined serif. The hero is concrete, not conceptual.
- The constellation background reinforces "person in a network" — a graduated alumni connected to the broader community.
- The arrow `→ MIT + ICLR` uses the WAT.AI directional vocabulary to compress an entire achievement into one line. Compression is the voice.
- The muted gold `01` behind the top-right tag does double duty: it's a series marker *and* a visual anchor that balances the asymmetric hero.
- The vertical accent bar on the left is the only structural decoration in the entire layout. It implies a series, like a magazine spine.

**Anti-pattern this avoids:** putting a photograph of the person. The *type* is the portrait. This is intentional — keeping the system photograph-free means it can scale to any subject without sourcing a usable headshot.

## What the three examples have in common

Recognize these as the invariants — they're what makes the system the system:

1. **Black canvas, white hero, gold metadata.** Always.
2. **Three type families, no more.** Outlined serif, heavy sans, monospace.
3. **Corner metadata, center hero.** The skeleton.
4. **Background coded to content type.** Not interchangeable.
5. **Compression over explanation.** `→ MIT + ICLR` instead of "now pursuing a PhD at MIT and presenting at ICLR." The voice trusts the reader.
6. **Photograph-free.** Type and atmosphere only.

A new graphic that holds all six is on-brand even if no existing post looks like it. A new graphic that breaks any of them needs justification.
